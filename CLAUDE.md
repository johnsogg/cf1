## Language
- **CF1 language:** JavaScript throughout
- **p5.js** at the start; other rendering approaches introduced ~W6 with local editing

## Weekly cadence
- **Monday:** Homework due before class; lecture continues current topic
- **Wednesday:** Quiz due before class (covers reading/prior material, not that
  day's lecture); lecture introduces new material
- **Friday:** Recitation — students get help with the upcoming homework

Exceptions: when Monday falls on a holiday, both homework and quiz shift to
Wednesday of that week (e.g., W3: Labor Day moves HW due date to Wed Sep 9).

## Projects
Three projects spaced across the semester:
1. Solo
2. Group
3. Student's choice (solo or group)

Grading is based on submitted work, not in-class presentation. In-class
demos/presentations are voluntary for projects 1 and 2, required for project 3 —
purpose is sharing and inspiring, not assessment.

## Course blocks
1. **Main block:** Learning to code; modeling ideas with software
2. **Ecosystem block:** Collaboration, tooling, working on real projects
3. **Launch block:** Setting students up for follow-on courses (data structures,
   web, object, game dev)

## Pedagogy note
The course is intentionally front-loaded — students must work hard from day one.
Pace is fast by design; previous semesters showed students disengage when the
opening weeks move too slowly.
